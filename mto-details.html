Handover Dyed II - OW

Details

This is one of our first ever One Washed Collection. It is the one-washed version of our Hand Overdyed II article. The fabric started out as an unsanforized 15 ounces indigo selvedge denim originated from Cone Mills USA. And then hand-dipped 11 times into Indonesian traditional indigo solvent.

The color evolved into a really nice, rich, and deep blue tone. as a 100% cotton denim, the weft and the selvedge line part were also had a color change. As all of this overdyed process is purely done by hand, you can see some uneven coloration part in the fabric. Finished with one-washed process resulting in a soft and very comfortable pair of jeans.

Our latest H and Overdued article use the Oldblue signature slim cut. Our 7.5” cut is a basic slim cut. It is very suitable for someone who looking for a real slim silhouette cut. It has a slightly lower rise comparing to our other cuts. Although it has a slim cut, we surely do not override the aspect of comfort.

For the pocket lining, we use the exact same fabric as the base material. And we use the selvedge line detail for it. For the hardware, we use Japan made 100% copper washer - burrrivets and “Laurel Leaf” iron plated doughnut button with black doff finish, also made in Japan. The laurel leaf is a symbol of peace, widely used to symbolize the triumph of the human spirit. Levi`s used such emblem as their waistband button during the world war II.

We use new design with new leather material for this anniversary edition. The material used is a bison hide finished with saddle color. The leather is directly imported from its origin, USA. It also varies in thickness, 3-4 ounces in average, or 1.4 - 1. 6 mm. Genuine American bison leather is more than 40% stronger than the traditional cowhide. The American bison hide is really famous with its softness, strength, and durability. Comparing to the cowhide, bison leather grain is more pronounced, and the fibers are also thicker.
